## Project Notes

### Spirit Camper
- Medium: Game Design, Unreal Engine 5
- Date: November 2024
- Walkthrough: [Spirit Camper gameplay](https://www.youtube-nocookie.com/embed/FUAOf7wHHUY)
- Assets: `assets/images/spirit-camper/`

Summary:
A narrative-driven adventure game built in Unreal Engine 5. The player explores a forest, interacts with characters and objects, gathers materials, and helps Seraphina create a cure for a deadly poison.

Key notes:
- first Unreal Engine 5 game project, built around exploration, dialogue, collectibles, and light environmental puzzles
- focused on player guidance through blocked paths, invisible walls, notes, and simple interface cues
- arranged environmental assets and implemented teleportation, NPC interaction, a key-and-door system, and AI-generated voice lines
- reflection centered on iteration, scope control, and expanding the story/mechanics later

### Sheldon
- Medium: Scratch, Photoshop, Micro:bit, Programming, Clay Sculpture
- Date: December 2024
- Walkthrough: [Sheldon gameplay](https://www.youtube-nocookie.com/embed/ht8jK31BSgM)
- Assets: `assets/images/sheldon/`

Summary:
Sheldon is a hybrid pet simulator that combines a clay turtle sculpture, micro:bit interaction, and a Scratch game. Players pet the turtle to raise its friendship meter, creating a simple and relaxing interactive experience.

Key notes:
- collaborative hybrid project combining a sculpted object with digital interaction
- Ray focused on Scratch gameplay/menu design, dialogue, reactions, AI-edited turtle visuals, and voice lines
- the clay turtle shell and belly used copper tape with micro:bit touch input
- reflection focused on playful physical-digital interaction and a desire to expand the story and cast

### Vampire Slayer
- Medium: Electronics, Programming, Micro:bits, Cardboard
- Date: November 2024
- Walkthrough: [Vampire Slayer walkthrough](https://www.youtube-nocookie.com/embed/KpaRr-BpMeA)
- Assets: `assets/images/vampire-slayer/`

Summary:
Vampire Slayer is a light-based interactive game where players use their phone flashlights to hit popping vampire heads. Inspired by Whack-a-Mole, the project combines micro:bits, motors, sensors, and a hand-built cardboard structure.

Key notes:
- started from brainstorming and prototyping with styrofoam plus a micro:bit motor
- evolved into a seesaw mechanism that raised and lowered vampire heads
- players shine phone flashlights onto heads to trigger light sensors and reset the mechanism
- Ray contributed build materials and focused on the red-and-black visual theme and presentation

### E.V.I.L.
- Medium: Board Game Design
- Date: March 2025
- Format: 3-player cooperative strategy game
- Assets: `assets/images/evil/`

Summary:
E.V.I.L. is a cooperative strategy board game for three players where villains fight through branching paths of heroes using coordinated ability cards. The project combines worldbuilding, game systems, and visual design into a team-based experience focused on strategy and timing.

Key notes:
- villains are centered as protagonists working through hero territory toward a Legendary Hero
- players choose from Mutant, Witch, Cyborg, and Zombie, using Strength, Magic, Tech, and Agility cards
- the system uses deck management, timing, Ultimate Attacks, and Companion cards
- Ray led concept development, MDA thinking, board map design, character sketches, written materials, and balance tweaks through playtesting

### The Heavy 20s
- Medium: Illustrator, Photoshop
- Date: November 2025
- Format: Individual magazine cover design
- Assets: `assets/images/the-heavy-20s/`

Summary:
The Heavy 20s is a minimalist magazine cover designed for an article about anxiety in young adults. Inspired by Noma Bar's graphic style, it uses bold typography and a simple visual metaphor to communicate two contrasting ideas within one editorial image.

Key notes:
- based on an article about anxiety, depression, and loneliness in young adulthood
- developed around the contrasting ideas of carefree and burden
- explored multiple sketch, layout, typography, and color directions before landing on the balloon-and-chain solution
- reflection emphasized typography, cropping, color contrast, spacing, and how a simple image can hold a complex idea

### Frottage
- Medium: Frottage Textures, Mixed Media
- Date: September 2024
- Format: Mixed media texture series
- Assets: `assets/images/frottage/`

Summary:
Frottage is a mixed media project that transforms texture rubbings from everyday surfaces into imaginative artworks. Using observation, pareidolia, and digital editing, it explores freedom, ascent, and unease.

Key notes:
- began from an interest in frottage as a storytelling method, not just pattern-making
- used rubbings from sidewalks, tree bark, and shoe soles as source material
- Photoshop refinement helped shape mood and final composition
- named works included `Flight of the Free`, `Heavenly Ascent`, and `The Staring Shadows`
