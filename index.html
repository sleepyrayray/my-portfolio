Project 01

Spirit Camper

  • Medium Game Design, Unreal Engine 5
  • Date November 2024
  • Video Embedded walkthrough below

A narrative-driven adventure game built in Unreal Engine 5. The player explores a forest, interacts with characters and objects, gathers materials, and helps Seraphina create a cure for a deadly poison.

Development Summary

This was my introduction to Unreal Engine 5 and digital game development. After exploring several ideas, I chose Spirit Camper for its narrative focus and built the project around exploration, dialogue, collectibles, and light environmental puzzles.

Design and Gameplay

I focused on player guidance and usability. Blocked paths, invisible walls, simple interface elements, and notes placed around the map helped guide players through the story clearly.

Technical and Creative Work

I sourced and arranged environmental assets such as trees, flowers, and a tombstone to shape the world and atmosphere. I also implemented teleportation, NPC interaction, and a key and door system, and used AI-generated voice lines for both characters.

Reflection

This project was challenging and rewarding because it was my first digital game in Unreal Engine 5. It taught me a lot about iteration, flexibility, and balancing scope with time. If I continued it, I would expand the story and add more mechanics.

Project 02

Sheldon

  • Medium Scratch, Photoshop, Micro:bit, Programming, Clay Sculpture
  • Date December 2024
  • Video Embedded walkthrough below

Sheldon is a hybrid pet simulator that combines a clay turtle sculpture, micro:bit interaction, and a Scratch game. Players pet the turtle to raise its friendship meter, creating a simple and relaxing interactive experience.

Development Summary

My teammate and I created Sheldon as a hybrid project that joined physical and digital interaction. Inspired by a classmate's turtle-themed design, we explored how a sculpted object and a digital game could work together through touch input and Scratch gameplay.

My Role

I focused on the digital side by designing and coding the gameplay and menu in Scratch, building the friendship meter, writing Sheldon's dialogue, and shaping his reactions. I also sourced and edited AI-generated turtle images and used AI-generated voice lines for sound.

Physical and Digital Link

My partner sculpted the turtle in clay and used copper tape on the shell and belly to connect it to the micro:bit. That setup made the turtle responsive to touch and let the player's physical interaction directly affect the game.

Reflection

I am proud of how this project turned out. It brought together sculpture, programming, and interaction in a playful way, and the mix of physical object and digital interface helped Sheldon feel engaging and complete. With more time, I would expand the story and add more characters.

Project 03

Vampire Slayer

  • Medium Electronics, Programming, Micro:bits, Cardboard
  • Date November 2024
  • Video Embedded walkthrough below

Vampire Slayer is a light-based interactive game where players use their phone flashlights to hit popping vampire heads. Inspired by Whack-a-Mole, the project combines micro:bits, motors, sensors, and a hand-built cardboard structure.

Development Summary

Our process began with brainstorming ideas and choosing the Whack-a-Mole format as the base for the project. We first prototyped the mechanics with styrofoam and a micro:bit motor, then refined the system into a seesaw mechanism that lifted and lowered the vampire heads.

How It Works

Players use their phone flashlights on the vampire heads as they appear. Each light sensor detects the flashlight and triggers the matching seesaw mechanism to lower the head back into the box.

My Contribution

I contributed materials for the build, including the large cardboard structure and coloured paper used for the game box. I also focused on the visual design, making sure the red and black vampire theme matched the mechanics and presentation.

Reflection

This project was a strong learning experience. My partner and I ran into challenges with the seesaw mechanism, but we improved it by adjusting the structure and movement system. Even with a few small issues, I was proud to see the idea work in physical form.
